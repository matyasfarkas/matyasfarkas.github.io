    External Block — the balance-of-payments satellite (Trade Balance + Current Account + NFA/GDP)¶

    The external block estimates the external drivers inside the BVAR (trade flows and the exchange rate) and derives the reporting variables — trade balance, current account, and the net international investment position (net foreign assets, NFA) as a share of GDP — by accounting identity. This is the exact same division of labour as the fiscal block: a reduced-form model is never allowed to freely forecast a stock (there, public debt; here, NFA). Instead the BVAR forecasts the flows and the FX index, and a separate, side-effect-free accounting module rolls the stock forward by the balance-of-payments identity, seeded from a real, sourced anchor.

    Together the fiscal block, this external block, and the monetary core complete the IMF FPP monetary–fiscal–external triad of sectoral consistency: fiscal rolls the debt stock from a budget identity, external rolls the NFA stock from the BoP identity, and both share the same two-layer pattern (BVAR flow generator + identity recursion) and the same %-of-GDP annual-ratio convention.

    The shared backbone (identical to the fiscal block)¶

    Both satellites separate "forecast the macro + sector flows in the BVAR" from "derive the sector stock by accounting." Neither lets the reduced form forecast the stock directly.

    • Fiscal: BVAR forecasts revenue/GDP, primary-expenditure/GDP, the effective rate and the SFA → app/src/lib/compute/fiscalAccounting.ts rolls debt/GDP forward by the FPP/DSA debt-dynamics identity, seeded by d0 in fiscal_meta.json.
    • External: BVAR forecasts exports, imports, GDP and the FX index → app/src/lib/compute/externalAccounting.ts rolls NFA/GDP forward by the BoP identity, seeded by nfa0 (real net-IIP / nominal GDP) in external_meta.json.

    The authoritative implementation is computeExternalBlock in app/src/lib/compute/externalAccounting.ts. It is read-only and side-effect-free: it consumes a level-space forecast path and returns per-quarter arrays of length H. It is wired into app/src/components/charts/ChartGrid.tsx exactly the way the fiscal block is — a derived useMemo, a per-draw band loop, and synthetic chart traces — and is fully gated on the presence of a chartable external_meta.json (a bloc without one renders nothing, with zero band work).

    Element-by-element mapping¶

    Concept IMF BoP / FPP external sector This tool
    Exports / imports (drivers) goods+services trade flows estimated BVAR level vars (US XH/MH; EA EXP_EA/IMP_EA)
    GDP (denominator) nominal GDP estimated BVAR level var (GDPH / GDP_EA)
    Exchange rate (driver) NEER / REER estimated BVAR log var (US FXTWM; EA NEER_EA)
    Trade balance exports − imports, % GDP derived TB = 100·(X−M)/gdp$
    Primary income on NFA investment income on the net IIP derived income = iNFA·NFA₋₁ (imputed return)
    Current account trade balance + primary income (+ transfers) derived CA = TB + income
    FX valuation effect revaluation of FX-denominated net assets derived val = −fxShare·NFA₋₁·ΔFX
    NFA / net IIP path NFAₜ = NFA₋₁ + CAₜ + valuationₜ derived recursion (the BoP stock identity)
    NFA seed observed net IIP / GDP sourced nfa0 in external_meta.json (real, per bloc)

    The NFA recursion is the standard BoP stock-flow identity — the net international investment position changes by the current-account flow plus valuation (revaluation) effects, the same decomposition the IMF/ECB external statistics and the Lane–Milesi-Ferretti External Wealth of Nations dataset use.

    The recursion, precisely¶

    Read the units block at the top of externalAccounting.ts before editing anything here. Per forecast quarter t = 0 … H−1, read off the level-space forecast path (100·log for log vars; level for lin):

    • exports / imports / GDP — log vars; the quantity is exp(level/100). Trade balance is a % of GDP, so the numerator (exports − imports) and the denominator gdp$ must be in a common nominal scale. For US (XH/MH/GDPH all real chained Bil USD) and EA (EXP_EA/IMP_EA/GDP_EA all real, common units) they are, so the ratio is real-trade-balance / real-GDP and validates against the published BoP figure.
    • the FX index (idx.fx, log var: US FXTWM, EA NEER_EA, CN REER_CN) — "up = home currency appreciates." A rise lowers the domestic-currency value of FX-denominated net assets, hence the negative valuation sign below.
    • iNFA (idx-level iNFA, % p.a.) — the imputed return on the NFA stock, a modeling default (~3%), flagged in external_meta.json (_flags).
    • fxShare (fxShare, fraction in [0,1]) — the FX-exposed share of the NFA stock, a modeling default (~0.4), flagged.

    The seed nfa0 (initial NFA/GDP, %) is the real, sourced net-IIP/GDP anchor from external_meta.json.

    Per-quarter recursion (using lagged NFA NFA₋₁ so the loop stays explicit)¶

    With NFA₋₁ = nfa0 at t=0 and NFA₋₁ = nfa[t−1] thereafter, and prev the previous level row (prevLevelRow at t=0, for the FX diff seam):

    1. Trade balance (% of GDP, annual ratio): TBₜ = 100·(exp(levelₜ[exports]/100) − exp(levelₜ[imports]/100)) / exp(levelₜ[gdp]/100) (externalAccounting.ts:104).
    2. Primary income on the NFA stock: incomeₜ = iNFA · NFA₋₁ / 100 (externalAccounting.ts:106). This is the imputed-return channel (modeling default), the only place iNFA enters.
    3. Current account (% of GDP, annual): CAₜ = TBₜ + incomeₜ (externalAccounting.ts:107).
    4. FX valuation effect this quarter: with dFXₜ = levelₜ[fx] − prev[fx] (Δ of 100·log FX), valₜ = −fxShare · NFA₋₁ · dFXₜ / 100 (externalAccounting.ts:109–110). A home appreciation (dFX>0) cuts the home value of FX-denominated net assets, so for a net creditor it reduces NFA and for a net debtor (nfa0<0) it raises NFA (FX-denominated net liabilities shrink).
    5. Roll the stock forward (the BoP stock identity), quarterly: NFAₜ = NFA₋₁ + CAₜ/4 + valₜ (externalAccounting.ts:112). The current account is an annual ratio so it enters as a quarter-share (/4); the valuation effect is already this quarter's.
    6. Derived reporting variable: Δ(NFA/GDP)ₜ = NFAₜ − NFA₋₁ (externalAccounting.ts:119).

    The recursion returns { tb, income, ca, valuation, nfa, dNfa }, all length H.

    Anchors and which blocs have an external block¶

    The seed nfa0 is never invented; it is a real, sourced net-IIP/GDP figure (per the absolute no-synthetic rule). A bloc whose IIP cannot be verified, or whose trade flows and GDP are not in a common scale, ships without a chartable external_meta.json and renders no external charts.

    Bloc External block? nfa0 (% GDP) Source TB anchor (h0)
    US Yes (renders) −66.75 FRED IIPUSNETIQ (US Net IIP, −21,270,237 Mil USD, 2026-01-01) / FRED GDP (nominal SAAR, 31,865.721 Bil USD, 2026Q1). US = large net debtor. −3.80% (deficit; published −3 to −4% range)
    EA Yes (renders) +11.0 ECB official release "Euro area quarterly balance of payments and international investment position: Q4 2025" — net assets EUR 1.76 tn = 11.0% of EA GDP. EA ≈ small net creditor. +3.59% (modest surplus, net exporter)
    CN Yes (renders, via unit reconciliation) +20.35 SAFE "China's IIP at end-2025" — net external assets USD 4,071.3 bn; NBS 2025 nominal GDP CNY 140.1879 tn = USD 20.01 tn (implied CNY/USD 7.006) ⇒ +20.35% GDP. Cross-check BoFiT ~20%. CN = net creditor. +4.46% (goods-trade surplus; published ~4–5% range)
    JP, UK, CA No (no external_meta.json) — IIP not yet verified for these blocs (flagged) —

    So US, EA and CN are the blocs that render the external block. CN required a units reconciliation (see caveat 5) because its trade flows and GDP are in incompatible native units; with the real, sourced conversion factors it now ships externalChartable:true. JP / UK / CA ship no external_meta.json at all (loader's Promise.allSettled resolves 404 → null → nothing renders, no band work).

    The iNFA (~3% p.a.) and fxShare (~0.4) parameters are modeling defaults shipped clearly flagged (the _flags key in each external_meta.json), exactly like the structural-constant SFA in the fiscal block — they are not estimated or sourced.

    Honest caveats (read before relying on the output)¶

    These are documented honestly because the tool is a live research instrument.

    1. The trade balance is a real-VOLUME balance, not the nominal customs CA (US, EA)¶

    For US and EA the trade flows and GDP are in a common real scale (US real chained Bil USD; EA real, common units), so the ratio is a real-trade-volume / real-GDP balance. It validates against the published nominal BoP trade balance to within a relative-price wedge (US ≈ −3.8%, EA ≈ +3.5%), but it is not the nominal customs/BoP current account to the decimal. Terms-of-trade swings between export and import prices are not captured because the BVAR carries real volumes, not separate export/import deflators. Treat the levels as economically faithful, the high-frequency wiggles as volume-driven.

    2. Primary income is IMPUTED, not estimated (iNFA)¶

    The primary-income leg of the current account is income = iNFA·NFA₋₁ with a modeling-default imputed return iNFA ≈ 3% p.a. — it is not an estimated BVAR variable and not sourced. It is flagged in external_meta.json (_flags), like the structural SFA. Because income compounds on the lagged NFA stock, a more-negative NFA path mechanically produces a more-negative income drag (and vice versa); this is a deliberate, transparent channel, but it is a parameterized assumption, not data.

    3. FX valuation uses an external parameter (fxShare)¶

    The valuation (revaluation) effect val = −fxShare·NFA₋₁·ΔFX uses a modeling-default FX-exposed share fxShare ≈ 0.4 — the fraction of the net IIP denominated in foreign currency. It is not sourced and is flagged like iNFA. The sign convention (home appreciation cuts the home value of FX net assets) means a fiscal expansion that appreciates the currency can, for a net debtor (nfa0<0, e.g. US), produce a positive valuation gain (FX-denominated net liabilities shrink) — an economically correct J-curve/valuation channel, kept ON in the live charts. The unconfounded twin-deficit signal is therefore read off the trade/CA flow in the medium-term window, not the long-horizon NFA stock, whose dynamics are valuation-rich.

    4. nfa0 is the EXTERNAL seed — real, sourced, per bloc¶

    The NFA recursion is only as anchored as its seed. nfa0 is a real net-IIP/GDP figure sourced per bloc (US: FRED IIPUSNETIQ/GDP; EA: ECB Q4-2025 IIP release; CN: SAFE end-2025 IIP). It is never invented. If a bloc's IIP cannot be verified it ships without an external block (JP/UK/CA today). After any re-estimation that changes a bloc's variable order, re-verify the positional idx in external_meta.json (exports/imports/gdp/deflator/fx) — the indices are positional and not auto-regenerated, the same staleness hazard the fiscal block flags.

    5. CN units reconciliation — incompatible native units brought to a common nominal-USD ratio¶

    CN is the one bloc whose trade flows and GDP are not in a common native scale, so the raw level paths cannot be ratioed directly (the un-converted TB/GDP is off by ~10⁵). The mismatch is purely one of units and frequency, not of missing data, so it is resolved by a small set of real, sourced conversion factors (units.* in external_meta.json) — no FX rate or flow is fabricated.

    Updated (surgical CN-only real-GDP re-estimate, 2026-06). GDP_CN is now a real chain-linked volume index (not nominal CNY), so the GDP denominator is reconstructed in nominal USD via the gdpKind:'realIndexDeflate' branch: gdp$ = exp(gdp/100)·exp(deflator/100)·usdBase, with a single calibrated usdBase = 158760958.8679 scalar (CN's external block carries tradeFreqMult: 3 and gdpKind:'realIndexDeflate'). This pins the CN goods-trade balance to the published +4.46% of GDP at the historical anchor. The legacy nominal-CNY path below (gdpScale/fxToUsd) is retained for historical reference only and is no longer the active conversion for CN. This was a CN-only graft; the other 32 GVAR blocs are byte-identical to the prior working model.

    The legacy native units, verified from the last historical level row (exp(level/100)):

    Series Native unit exp(level/100) (last row) Conversion
    GDP_CN (legacy nominal) nominal CNY, millions, quarterly 35,984,541 (≈ 36 tn CNY/quarter) gdpScale=1e6 (millions → ones), then ÷ fxToUsd (CNY → USD)
    EXP_CN nominal USD, monthly-average flow 314,673,049,926 (≈ $315 bn/month) × tradeFreqMult=3 (monthly → quarterly)
    IMP_CN nominal USD, monthly-average flow 239,543,292,627 (≈ $240 bn/month) × tradeFreqMult=3 (monthly → quarterly)

    The factors:

    • usdBase = 158760958.8679 (active) — realIndexDeflate scalar that maps the real chain-linked exp(gdp/100)·exp(deflator/100) product into nominal USD, calibrated so the CN trade balance lands at the published +4.46% of GDP at the anchor.
    • gdpScale = 1e6 (legacy nominal path) — when GDP_CN was in millions of CNY; multiplying exp(gdp/100) by 1e6 put the denominator in ones (CNY), matching the trade flows in ones (USD).
    • fxToUsd = 6.7686 (legacy nominal path) — the real CNY/USD spot rate; GDP-in-CNY ÷ this = GDP-in-USD. Sourced from FRED series DEXCHUS, latest non-missing observation 2026-06-18. Cross-confirmed against its primary upstream source — the Federal Reserve H.10 Foreign Exchange Rates release (federalreserve.gov/releases/h10/current, release dated 2026-06-22, China spot for 2026-06-18), a byte-exact match. Not synthetic.
    • tradeFreqMult = 3 — EXP_CN/IMP_CN are monthly-average USD flows while GDP is quarterly; ×3 quarterizes the flows so numerator and denominator share a frequency.

    Applied to the recursion (externalAccounting.ts):

    gdp$  = exp(gdp/100) · gdpScale / fxToUsd          // CNY-millions → USD
exp$  = exp(exports/100) · tradeFreqMult           // monthly-USD → quarterly-USD
imp$  = exp(imports/100) · tradeFreqMult
TB    = 100 · (exp$ − imp$) / gdp$                 // % of GDP, common nominal-USD ratio

    which gives, at the forecast anchor, TB ≈ +4.46% of GDP — squarely in China's published goods-trade surplus range (~4–5% of GDP). CN therefore now ships externalChartable:true and renders the external block on this common-USD ratio.

    These factors are pure unit/frequency conversions, not data. Each native series keeps its own real sourced value; the factors only restate them in a single nominal-USD, quarterly scale. The default for any bloc that ships no units.* (US, EA) is gdpScale=fxToUsd=tradeFreqMult=1, so the US/EA paths are byte-identical to before this change (guarded by the byte-identity test). The CN factors are load-bearing — a test asserts that without them the ratio is absurd (>10³) — so a future editor must never drop them back to the raw level paths. The CN units behaviour is pinned by app/src/__tests__/compute/externalAccounting.test.ts.

    6. The displayed NFA path is a PASSIVE accounting recursion (no debt/NFA feedback)¶

    Like the fiscal block's deployed computeFiscalBlock, the external recursion has no endogenous feedback from the NFA stock back onto the BVAR drivers (no NFA→FX risk premium, no NFA→trade adjustment). The trade flows and FX are debt-agnostic BVAR baselines; the income and valuation legs layer on the lagged stock with no double-counting. This is a transparent passive recursion, not a sustainability model — a possible future extension.

    Bands¶

    The external bands (TB / CA / NFA at q16/q84) are a faithful clone of the fiscal default-mode band: they are computed per-draw via computeExternalBlock on the unconditional level path, parallel-shifted by (scenarioMean − baselineMean), and injected into the three chart traces. They are cleared everywhere the fiscal bands are. The synthetic chart bvarIdx is 9100+si (distinct from the fiscal 9000+) with draggable:null, so the j-indexed conditioning / band / forecast loops skip it — the external path is strictly additive and the δc=0 + external no-op stays byte-exact with the fiscal no-op.

    Placement in one line¶

    A compact, interactive realization of the IMF FPP external sector: the BVAR forecasts the trade flows and the exchange rate, and a passive balance-of-payments stock-flow recursion rolls NFA/GDP forward from a real, sourced net-IIP seed (trade balance + imputed primary income + FX valuation). It is the external leg of the monetary–fiscal–external triad, built on the identical two-layer pattern as the fiscal block, shipped for US, EA and CN (CN via a real, sourced nominal-USD units reconciliation; JP/UK/CA pending verified IIP).
