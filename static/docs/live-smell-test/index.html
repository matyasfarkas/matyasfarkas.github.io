    Branch: improve-live @ 89c4501 (LIVE deployed) · Model: RW-BVAR, no target-reversion, no Taylor reaction · Horizon: h20 (terminal display) · Read-only diagnostic.

    This document grades every deployed economy on the central (terminal) forecast against economic-plausibility bands and confirms whether the UK/CA pathologies flagged by the user are systemic across the model set. All values are terminal-h forecasts. log columns are q/q-ann %, lin columns are levels/rates. Snowball = r − g where r = EFFI if a fiscal block exists, else the policy rate.

    2. Systemic Findings¶

    The UK/CA pattern flagged in the brief is not idiosyncratic — it is the dominant failure mode of the deployed model. Counts over the 51 economies:

    Inflation not anchoring (terminal >3.5% advanced / clearly off-target EM)¶

    25 of 51 (49%) smell on inflation: AU(3.07, above 3% band), CA, CL, CN(deflation −1.21), CO, CZ, EA, EE, HU, IE, IS, IT(too-low 0.69), LT, LU, LV, MX, NZ, PL, RU, SI, SK, TR, UK, US, plus CR/ID/FI on the low side (excluded from the hard count). Of these, the inflation drifts UP and stays high (the RW non-reversion signature) in ~20; a smaller cluster (CN, IT, AT, FI) drifts implausibly low/deflationary — confirming the drift runs in both directions, not just upward.

    Negative real rate (i − π < −0.5, no Taylor reaction)¶

    21 of 51 (41%): AT(−1.02), CA(−0.93), CH(−1.02), DE(−2.19), DK(−2.00), EA(−1.07), EE(−4.73), IL(−0.66), JP(−1.62), KR(−0.74), LT(−10.09), LU(−4.09), LV(−1.31), NO(−0.60), NZ(−1.87), PT(−0.50), SE(−0.50), SI(−1.90), SK(−2.85), UK(−1.74). (US escapes — its policy rate happens to stay above inflation.) Where a credible nominal-rate path exists it is by luck of the frozen terminal level, not policy logic.

    Debt snowball << −1.5 (debt falls implausibly)¶

    30 of 51 (59%) — the single most pervasive smell: AU, CA, CH, CL, CN, CZ, DE, DK, EA, EE, FR, ID, IE, IL, IN, JP, KR, LT, LU, LV, MY, NL, NO, NZ, PT, SA, SE, SI, SK, TW, UK, US (32 by the strict −1.5 cut; ~30 are the implausible-direction subset after netting EM growth-driven cases). For high-debt sovereigns where consensus is rising (FR d0~116, JP, EA, UK, US, NL, PT) a sustained r−g << −1.5 is squarely implausible. Three small EA members (EE −8.26, SI −8.69, LT −9.97) show outright debt collapse.

    Cross-cutting¶

    • 17 economies carry all three core nominal smells (non-anchored inflation and negative real rate and snowball << −1.5) or the EA-common-rate equivalent: CA, CH, DE-adjacent, EA, EE, LT, LU, LV, NZ, SI, SK, UK among them. These are the textbook RW-no-reversion cluster.
    • Real-side drift (no mean reversion in growth/unemployment) is the secondary failure: growth flatlines near 0 (AR, AT, GR −0.94, IT −0.22, HU −0.46) or runs implausibly hot (SA ~8, MY ~6.9, TW ~4.9, US 3.45, IN 7.86); unemployment drifts to absurd levels (SA → 0.1, FI 11.6, GR 12.4 drifting up, CH 6.2 vs true ~2-3).
    • The model is internally honest only where the data's terminal level happens to be sensible (BE, ES, TH, ZA, BR, MX, ID-rates) — i.e. the "clean" blocs pass by coincidence of the frozen RW endpoint, not by any anchoring mechanism.

    Verdict: the UK/CA observation is systemic. ~half the set fails inflation-anchoring, ~40% show negative real rates, and ~60% show implausibly favorable debt dynamics — all traceable to the same two missing mechanisms.

    3. Worst Offenders¶

    Ranked by number and severity of smells:

    1. EE (Estonia) — worst in set. Inflation 4.68 (volatile −3.81→+4.68, no anchor), real rate −4.73, snowball −8.26 → debt collapses from d0~24. Every band fails except unemployment.
    2. SI (Slovenia) — inflation drifts back UP to 5.71 (no glide to 2%), growth 5.2 >> potential, real rate −1.9, snowball −8.69 → debt collapse.
    3. LT (Lithuania) — inflation 10.27 oscillating (−13 to +14), real rate −10.09, snowball −9.97, nominal growth 12.4 absurd. Pure RW artifact contaminating everything.
    4. SK (Slovakia) — worst-anchored EA inflation 7.32, real rate −2.85, snowball −6.87.
    5. CA (Canada) — the brief's textbook case. Inflation 3.69 sticks, policy 2.77 < π → real rate −0.93, snowball −1.97 → debt falls vs CA consensus.
    6. UK (United Kingdom) — canonical pathology. Inflation 4.35 never glides to 2% BoE target, real rate −1.74, EFFI 3.32 << nominal growth 6.39 → snowball −3.07, debt falls vs consensus-rising.
    7. US (United States) — inflation 4.13 drifts up, growth 3.45 above potential, nominal growth 7.58 >> EFFI 4.16 → snowball −3.41; debt-fall masked by SFA drift to +14pp.
    8. EA (Euro Area aggregate) — inflation 3.32 RISES through horizon, real rate −1.07, snowball −3.12.
    9. NZ (New Zealand) — inflation RISES monotonically 2.83→5.45, real rate −1.87, snowball −3.87.
    10. CH / DK / DE — the negative-rate cluster: frozen near historical negative-rate regimes (CH −0.43, DK −0.51, DE 0.71 vs 2.9 π), real rates −1.0 to −2.2, snowballs −2.8 to −4.0.

    Opposite-direction offenders (drift to a bad equilibrium): GR (growth −0.94, unemployment drifts 8.4→12.4, snowball +1.29 = debt RISING vs consensus-falling), IT (inflation 0.69, growth −0.22, nominal growth ~0), SA (unemployment → 0.1). These prove the drift is not a one-sided upward-inflation bias but a generic absence of any attractor.

    4. Root Cause & Lightweight Live Fixes¶

    Root cause¶

    The deployed model is a random-walk-in-levels BVAR with a Minnesota/RW prior whose constant row acts as a drift, not a steady-state intercept. Two specific absences produce every smell above:

    1. No target-reversion for inflation. With a unit-root companion and no long-run mean, the terminal forecast is whatever the recent sample drifted to. Nothing pulls π toward 2% (advanced) or the EM target → inflation sticks high (UK, CA, NZ, EA…) or drifts low (CN deflation, IT) with equal ease.
    2. No monetary reaction function. The policy rate is a free RW series, not a function of the inflation/output gap. When π drifts up, the rate does not follow → systematically negative real rates (21 economies).

    These compound in the debt block: high un-anchored nominal growth − a frozen-low EFFI/policy rate → snowball << −1.5 → debt falls implausibly (30 economies). The real-side analog (no reversion in growth/unemployment) gives the flatline/hot-growth/unemployment-drift smells.

    Lightweight LIVE-version fix options¶

    (Not the full trend-cycle rebuild — that is parked on feature/trend-cycle-longrun-targets.) These are bolt-ons to the deployed RW-BVAR, ordered by effort:

    • (A) Per-country long-run prior tilt (cheapest, post-estimation). Entropic/PLR tilt of the existing posterior draws toward a per-country terminal target vector (π = target, i = π + neutral real rate, g = potential, u = NAIRU). Lives entirely in the projection layer; no re-estimation. Risk per MEMORY/PLR-vs-tilt: joint multi-constraint tilt degenerates (ESS→1) at 500 draws — apply per-constraint* (inflation-only, then rate) rather than a joint 13-moment tilt.
    • (B) Taylor-rule overlay on the rate path (targeted). Replace the free RW rate forecast with a reaction rule i_t = r* + π_t + φ_π(π_t − π*) + φ_y·ỹ_t evaluated on the model's own π/output draws. Fixes the 21 negative-real-rate blocs and feeds a sane r into the snowball, without touching the macro block. Cheapest fix for the debt-falls-implausibly smell on rate-proxy economies.
    • (C) Target-reverting inflation prior (medium, in-prior). Add a soft long-run mean (a "sum-of-coefficients"-style or explicit mean-reversion dummy) so π glides to π* over the horizon. This is the most economically honest of the bolt-ons but requires a (cheap) re-estimation per country.
    • (D) Combination A+B (tilt the macro terminal targets + Taylor-overlay the rate) addresses inflation-anchoring, negative-real-rate, and debt-snowball simultaneously with no macro-block re-estimation — the recommended interim while the trend-cycle rebuild matures.

    Recommendation (original, superseded — see the status update below): ship (B) Taylor overlay first (mechanical, no re-estimation, kills the negative-real-rate + a large share of the snowball smells), then layer (A) per-constraint inflation tilt for anchoring. Reserve (C) for the next re-estimation cycle. Defer to the parked trend-cycle VAR for the structural cure.

    Status update (implemented). Option (A) shipped as a deterministic δc drift overlay, not the entropic tilt — per-variable "Long-run targets" sliders that shift the companion drift so a variable's terminal forecast hits a user target (instant, exact, band-coherent; no re-estimation, no draw reweighting). It replaces the tilt (which degenerated, ESS→1) and sidesteps the per-constraint workaround entirely. The policy rate carries its own long-run-target slider like every other variable — set its terminal value directly through the drift setter. Option (B) Taylor overlay was not retained: an early taylorOverlay policy-rate-row toggle was shipped briefly and then removed (commit 9142265) — it was confusing and inert next to the rate's own slider; no taylorOverlay/setTaylorOverlay symbol remains. The perverse/near-singular policy-rate sliders (BE, CA, CZ) are instead disabled + labelled (the |sens| < 0.3 guard), not routed through any overlay. See MAINTAINER_OPERATIONS.md §G4 and USER_GUIDE.md §14. Option (C) (in-prior reversion) and the structural trend-cycle rebuild remain the next-cycle work.
