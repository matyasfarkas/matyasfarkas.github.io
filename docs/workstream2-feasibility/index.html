    GVAR panel-balance impact + estimator design¶

    The WEO-balancing is a convenience (rectangular per-bloc matrix), NOT an estimator requirement. cgl_bvarV5.m fits GLP coefficients only on xx(1:lastFull,:) (the last fully-observed row, line 39); remNaNs_prev method 6 leaves trailing NaNs and the DK simulation smoother imputes them — so a bloc truncated to genuine-end + NaN-padded is estimated exactly as single-country to that end (same mechanism as the COVID mask). The GVAR-EM star-completion (buildstars) renormalises trade weights over the partners present each quarter, so a partner ending 2024Q1 just drops from the star sums for later quarters. Two designs: (A) ragged-in-place (smoother extrapolates the short tail into partners' stars — flag as model-implied) vs (B) single-country to genuine end + couple at solve-time (preferred for RU/KR — no fabricated foreign feed). Recommended mix: re-source KR+ID to 2026Q1 (FRED/OECD), truncate RU/TH/IN/CN to genuine end via (B).

    1. Can the GVAR be re-estimated with the 6 truncated? — YES; the estimator handles ragged natively¶

    The estimator already drops a trailing NaN block (verified in cgl_bvarV5.m): - lastFull = find(~isnan(sum(x,2)),1,'last') (line 39) → the GLP coefficient fit uses only xx(1:lastFull,:) (lines 54, 162-163, 183). A trailing NaN block is excluded from the likelihood. - remNaNs_prev method 6 (Support/remNaNs_prev.m:135-146) carries previous values only between each column's first/last obs (t1:t2); trailing NaNs after a column's last obs are LEFT NaN and the DK simulation smoother (Xcond, lines 121-205) imputes them; the forecast splice starts at full T (lines 195-197). So a bloc truncated to its genuine end + NaN-padded to the common grid is estimated exactly as single-country to that end, with the smoother extrapolating the pad. Same mechanism the COVID mask uses — not a new code path.

    Does GVAR-EM / DK star-completion handle a 2024-Q1 end (KR/RU, 7–8q behind)? Yes, by construction. buildstars (export_bvar_gvar24_loop.m:177-205) intersects partner quarters per period (tp=find(D.(pn).dates==q,1)) and renormalises trade weights over the partners present each quarter (wd/wi/wf accumulate only available partners). A partner ending 2024-Q1 simply drops out of every star sum for 2024Q2–2025Q4; the rest renormalise. No balanced-panel requirement at the star layer — build_gvar_2025q4.py WEO-balances purely for a rectangular per-bloc matrix, not an estimator constraint.

    Two viable designs: - (A) Ragged-in-place: drop the WEO extension, feed genuine-then-NaN; the EM loop's buildstars renormalises per quarter; the DK smoother extrapolates each short bloc's tail. Risk: RU/KR 2024Q2–2025Q4 becomes pure smoother extrapolation propagated into other blocs' stars (a model-implied, not observed, foreign block for 7–8q). Acceptable only if flagged as model-implied. - (B) Single-country to genuine end, coupled at solve-time (preferred for RU/KR): estimate each short bloc to its own dates, build stars over the partner overlap. No fabricated foreign feed; the honest ragged edge. Already how dates are keyed per bloc.

    Net: re-estimable with all 6 truncated. Preferred mix — re-source KR + ID to 2026Q1 (FRED mirror), truncate RU/TH/IN/CN to genuine end via (B). RU is the only genuinely short/early case.
