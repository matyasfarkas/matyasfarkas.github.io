    MP Committee — Live Demo Script (15 min, laptop only, no slides)¶

    A timed, click-by-click runbook for the live tool demo. Each segment gives the exact clicks, the one-line econometric point to say out loud, and the expected on-screen result. Language rule: every conditional path is "the most likely projection given the conditioning assumptions" (a Waggoner–Zha / DK conditional forecast). Never say "entropic tilting."

    Pre-flight checklist (do this before the room)¶

    • [ ] Tool open in the browser, live build loaded (production bvarscenario/main).
    • [ ] Country = United States (top-right Country dropdown, GVAR optgroup; US is the default on load).
    • [ ] Units toggle = "q/q ann." — NOT "% vs baseline" (that view is read-only and disables dragging).
    • [ ] Anchors confirmed ON: left ControlPanel → "Long-run targets" header reads "(7 set)". If the debt path looks flat, anchors haven't seeded yet (async fetch of default_anchors.json) — refresh and wait a beat.
    • [ ] Clean scenario: ControlPanel header → "Reset all" → "Yes, reset all" once, so no stray pins and spillover switches are OFF.
    • [ ] Know where the financial charts live: 10Y (GLB_USLR), Baa (GLB_USBAA), oil (GLB_OIL), equity (GLB_USEQ) are in the "Global commons" section near the bottom. Aaa corporate (FAAA) and domestic WTI (PZTEXP) are in the "Country-specific variables" section. Scroll down once now so you can find them fast.
    • [ ] Between cases use "Reset" (clears this scenario's pins, keeps anchors). Only use "Reset all" when you want spillover switches OFF too.

    (0:00–0:30) — What it is¶

    "This is a multi-country Bayesian VAR forecasting tool with a fiscal satellite and a GVAR cross-country layer. Everything you'll see is live: I open the central projection, then build scenarios by conditioning, and export the whole thing to Excel."

    (0:30–4:00) — UNCONDITIONAL / central projection¶

    Monetary block (0:30–2:00)¶

    Clicks: The grid is already on US. Row 1 (macro triad) holds Inflation (source JGDP, "GDP Chain Price Index"), Policy Rate (POLRATE, "Effective Federal Funds Rate"), and GDP Growth (GDPH).

    Say (one-liner — rates vs levels): "Price indices like JGDP are modeled as 100·log price levels with a Minnesota / Litterman random-walk prior; what you see on the Inflation chart is a display transform — 4·Δ of that log level, i.e. q/q annualized. The policy rate and yields, by contrast, are modeled directly as levels in percent. So inflation is a transform of a modeled price level; the policy rate is a modeled level itself."

    Also say (prior + COVID, one line each): "Estimation is GLP — Giannone–Lenza–Primiceri — Minnesota prior shrinking toward a random walk. And the 2020 COVID quarters are treated as missing data, imputed inside the Gibbs sampler, so the pandemic outliers don't contaminate the dynamics."

    Expected on screen: Inflation glides to ~2%, policy rate toward neutral. Frame it: "the central projection with long-run targets anchored to consensus" — the consensus long-run anchors (the δc forward-pass: inflation→2%, policy→neutral, fiscal→OBR/IMF/CBO) are ON by default and are a deterministic drift, separate from any scenario conditioning. (If asked to show the raw BVAR: in a panel click "reset all to estimate" — inflation/debt revert to the drifting RW path; re-apply with "Apply consensus anchors.")

    Fiscal satellite (2:00–3:15)¶

    Clicks: Scroll to Row 2 — the 4 estimated BVAR fiscal variables, each its own chart: Revenue (FREV_GDP), Primary Expenditure (FPEXP_GDP), Effective Interest Rate (EFFI), Stock-Flow Adjustment (SFA). Directly under them: the header "Fiscal block — derived by accounting identity" with 3 derived charts: Primary Balance, Δ Debt-to-GDP, Debt-to-GDP.

    Say (one-liner — the satellite): "Government debt is not a BVAR variable. The BVAR estimates only these four — revenue, primary spending, the effective interest rate on the stock, and the stock-flow-adjustment residual. Debt is derived by rolling them through the budget identity: dₜ = dₜ₋₁·(1+i)/(1+g) − pb/4 + sfa/4, seeded at d₀ = 122.6% of GDP."

    Say (the snowball, one line): "(1+i)/(1+g) is the snowball — when the effective rate exceeds nominal growth, debt rises even with a balanced primary budget; pb = revenue − primary spending is the discretionary effort; SFA is the deficit-debt residual — below-the-line and valuation items the headline deficit misses."

    Expected on screen: Debt-to-GDP rises realistically (not a flat degenerate path) — because the consensus anchors hold inflation at ~2%, the effective rate near neutral (EFFI 5.0), and revenue/spending at CBO ratios. The four WN-prior fiscal ratios mean-revert (revenue ~17.5%) rather than drift, which is what keeps the path defensible.

    Excel export (3:15–4:00)¶

    Clicks: First set Uncertainty = Quick (TopBar) and press Compute so the bands populate. Then on the bottom ScenarioBar click the Excel button.

    Say: "It downloads forecast_US_<name>_<timestamp>.xlsx — one sheet per variable, columns Quarter | Date | Uncond. Q16 | Baseline (median) | Uncond. Q84 | scenario Q16 | scenario median | scenario Q84 | Deviation, plus a Summary sheet and a Notes sheet documenting each source variable and unit."

    Expected on screen: File downloads; every monetary and fiscal variable (including derived Debt-to-GDP) has its own tab. (Scenario columns are blank until a scenario is computed — that comes next.)

    (4:00–8:00) — CASE 1: GFC corporate-yield shock (Waggoner–Zha)¶

    Story: pull the corporate yield up. First without pinning the govt 10Y (flight-to-quality), then with it pinned unchanged (clean credit-spread shock).

    Step 1 — corporate up, 10Y FREE (4:00–6:00)¶

    Clicks: Scroll to "Country-specific variables" → Moody's Seasoned Aaa Corporate Bond Yield (FAAA). Click the panel's "condition" button (turns blue → "CONDITIONED"). Drag the line UP ~+150–200 bp in the early quarters (t+1..t+4), or right-click a quarter to plant the bullet exactly at the cursor value. Do not touch the 10Y. Press Compute (Quick).

    Say (one-liner): "Only the corporate yield is pinned, so the Treasury 10Y is free. This is the most likely projection given that one conditioning assumption — and the model pulls the risk-free 10Y down: flight-to-quality, with output and inflation softening. A recession read."

    Expected on screen: Corporate yield up where you pinned it; the 10Y (GLB_USLR, Global commons) drifts down; growth/inflation soften.

    Step 2 — ADD 10Y held UNCHANGED (6:00–8:00)¶

    Clicks: Scroll to "Global commons" → 10-Year Treasury Note Yield (GLB_USLR; conditionable because Country==US). Click its "condition" button but do not move the line — a fresh condition starts at the baseline (zero-deviation), which is "held unchanged." Press Compute again.

    Say (one-liner): "Now both yields are pinned — corporate up, risk-free curve held flat. That isolates a pure credit-spread / financial-conditions shock with no flight-to-quality offset. The clean scenario."

    Expected on screen: Corporate up, 10Y flat at baseline; the spread blowout is now the whole story. (Tip: per-quarter sliders in the left ControlPanel "Conditioning" section let you pin only the early impact window and leave the tail free; "All" toggles all 20 quarters.)

    (8:00–11:30) — CASE 2: EA-yield shock → US & global spillovers (GVAR)¶

    "Reset, switch to the Euro Area, shock the same yield there, and watch it reach the US through the cross-country coupling."

    Clicks: 1. ControlPanel header → "Reset all" → "Yes, reset all" (clean, not stacked on the GFC; also returns spillover switches to OFF — we turn them back on deliberately). 2. Top-bar Country dropdown → Euro Area (GVAR-linked section). 3. Shock the same yield you used for the US: EA 10Y Gov Bond Yield (YIELD10_EA) for the govt version, or EA IG Corporate Bond Yield (EACORP) for the corporate version. Right-click the chart at the target quarter/level (right-click conditions AND plants the bullet in one step), then drag up ~+100–150 bp across the first few quarters. 4. Left ControlPanel → "Cross-border spillovers" → set "Scenario spillovers (GVAR solve)" to On. 5. Click "▶ Solve full GVAR" for the complete coupled fixed point (all feedback loops, RoW→US).

    Say (one-liner): "The GVAR links blocs through three trade-weighted RoW star variables per country — foreign demand, foreign inflation, foreign short rate. The EA shock raises EA's contribution to every partner's stars, and the solver iterates the coupled fixed point x = Mx + d. A single-country EA BVAR would hold the US fixed; the GVAR lets EA tightening pull on US financial conditions and global activity. This is where the cross-country coupling shines."

    Expected on screen (two read-outs): - Stay on EA and watch the in-model Global commons charts move: US 10Y (GLB_USLR), US BAA (GLB_USBAA), US equity / S&P 500 (GLB_USEQ), WTI oil (GLB_OIL). - Switch Country → United States — the EA pins persist across the switch (separate store slice), so the full US bloc (growth, inflation, policy rate, yields) shows the EA→US spillover even though the US has no own pins.

    Gotcha to remember: without turning the spillover switch On (or pressing Solve full GVAR), EA moves alone and the US/global charts won't budge — this is the #1 thing to not forget. A full 50-bloc solve is ~4.5s warm; press once and let it land.

    (11:30–14:30) — CASE 3: US oil/energy — demand vs supply¶

    "Reset first — this is NOT on top of the GFC. Condition only on higher oil → demand dominates. Then add short-term Cholesky-type sign restrictions to flip it to an adverse supply shock."

    Setup clicks: Country back to United States. ControlPanel header → "Reset" (clears this scenario's pins; keeps the consensus anchors — exactly what we want). Confirm all "condition" buttons are grey.

    Step 1 — oil UP alone → DEMAND-dominant (11:30–13:00)¶

    Clicks: Use the US-domestic oil series Spot Oil Price: West Texas Intermediate (PZTEXP, "Country-specific variables"). Click "condition", then drag the line UP to a higher level (e.g. ~\(70 → ~\)100). It's a level-display var, so you're setting a $/bbl level, not a growth rate — drag to the actual target price. Press Compute (Quick).

    Say (one-liner): "With only oil pinned, the conditional solve reads the historical oil–output covariance as demand-driven — so US output rises with the oil price. The demand-dominant response."

    Expected on screen: Oil at the pinned level; US output up.

    Step 2 — add sign restrictions → ADVERSE SUPPLY (13:00–14:30)¶

    Clicks: Keep oil conditioned up. Now also condition GDP Growth (GDPH) and drag it DOWN at t+1..t+2; condition Inflation (JGDP) and drag it UP at t+1..t+2. Use the per-quarter sliders / "All"-off-then-enable-early so only t+1..t+2 are active and the tail stays free. Press Compute.

    Say (one-liner): "The 'Cholesky-type' move here is multi-variable Waggoner–Zha conditioning that pins the early signs: output down, inflation up in the impact window. That forces the supply-shock identification — a stagflationary response — instead of the demand read. The later quarters stay free, so the model fills in the dynamics."

    Expected on screen: Same oil rise, but now output down and inflation up early — stagflation. The contrast with Step 1 is the whole point.

    (14:30–15:00) — Close + Excel¶

    Clicks: After the final Compute, hit the ScenarioBar Excel button once more.

    Say: "Every case exports the same way — one sheet per variable, organized by date, with the unconditional baseline and ±1-s.d. band sitting next to the conditional path, band and deviation — including the satellite's derived Debt-to-GDP. The Summary tab lines all variables up. That's the full forecasting system: a central projection anchored to consensus, scenario conditioning on top, cross-country spillovers via the GVAR, and a one-click Excel hand-off."

    "If asked" — FAQ¶

    • The prior? "Minnesota / Litterman, GLP (Giannone–Lenza–Primiceri) implementation, shrinking toward a random walk on the macro and financial variables. The three fiscal ratios — revenue, primary spending, SFA — instead carry a white-noise prior so they revert to a long-run mean rather than drift; that's what keeps revenue at ~17.5% and stops the debt path running away."
    • Weak exogeneity of the RoW stars? "Each bloc's three trade-weighted foreign aggregates (ROWDEM/ROWINF/ROWFIN) enter as weakly exogenous regressors in the country VAR — standard Pesaran-type GVAR identification. At solve time the blocs are coupled and the foreign aggregates become endogenous to the system; we solve the fixed point x = Mx + d (closed form (I−M)⁻¹d), so consistency holds across the coupled solution. The US is endogenous in the live system with its own RoW stars."
    • COVID treatment? "2020 quarters are treated as missing data, imputed inside the Gibbs sampler — data augmentation in cgl_bvarV5, not Lenza–Primiceri volatility scaling. The outliers never enter the likelihood for the dynamics or the prior."
    • Anchors on vs raw? "The consensus long-run anchors are a deterministic δc forward-pass drift to publicly-defensible targets (inflation→2%, policy→neutral, fiscal→OBR/IMF/CBO). They're on by default, separate from the Waggoner–Zha conditioning, and toggle off via the 'Long-run targets' panel ('reset all to estimate') to show the raw drifting RW-BVAR."
    • Does the BVAR forecast debt? "No — it forecasts the four fiscal drivers; the satellite accumulates debt by the budget identity. The debt/PB bands come from running the recursion per-draw."

    Refined boss-reply (4 sentences)¶

    The 15-minute script is written to docs/mp-presentation-prep.md and follows your exact flow: (0:30–4:00) the unconditional US central projection — monetary block (inflation as a 4·Δ display transform of a 100·log price level vs the policy rate modeled directly as a level, Minnesota/GLP prior, COVID-as-missing) and the fiscal satellite (4 BVAR drivers → snowball/primary-balance/SFA debt recursion, now rising realistically with consensus anchors on by default), plus the per-variable Excel export. Case 1 is the GFC corporate-yield Waggoner–Zha scenario — FAAA up with the 10Y free (flight-to-quality), then GLB_USLR pinned unchanged (clean credit-spread shock); Case 2 is the EA-yield shock (YIELD10_EA/EACORP) with the GVAR spillover switch and "Solve full GVAR" showing the US/global response via the trade-weighted RoW stars; Case 3 resets and runs US oil (PZTEXP) demand-dominant alone, then early-quarter output↓/inflation↑ sign restrictions to flip it to an adverse supply shock. Throughout it's framed as "the most likely projection given the conditioning assumptions" — never entropic tilting — with the anchors kept separate from the scenario layer, and it ends on the per-variable/per-date Excel hand-off.
