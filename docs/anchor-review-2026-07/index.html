    Consensus-anchor review — July 2026¶

    Outcome: the shipped default anchor set shrinks from 104 anchors across 46 blocs to 32 anchors across 23 blocs. Every kept anchor fixes a genuine long-run pathology and leaves the near-term forecast inside the economy's own historical experience; everything else is dropped — either because the model already lands at consensus, or because the correction did more damage than the bias it corrected. The pruning was adversarially verified: three independent agents re-derived every sampled verdict exactly from the shipped files, and a referee pass found (and we fixed) three false drops and two blind spots before shipping.

    Why anchors exist — and why they are not innocuous¶

    The companions are estimated on short samples of highly persistent data. Near the unit root, autoregressive coefficients are biased downward in finite samples (Hurwicz/Kendall bias), and in a VAR with an intercept the level must be carried by something: the constant absorbs it, so the implied unconditional mean — the model's long-run terminal — is biased upward. The shipped terminals show it plainly (US deflator terminal 4.3 against 2.0 consensus; US nominal growth ≈ 8, which made the debt ratio melt away at i−g ≈ −3.9).

    The consensus anchors correct that bias — but the correction (δc) is a rigid translation of the whole path, not a change in dynamics. A large terminal gap buys anchor exactness with a front-loaded near-term shift (the July 2026 episode: the joint solve pushed displayed US inflation negative for two forecast years). Correcting the upward bias is not innocuous, so the shipped set must be the minimum that resolves the worst pathologies without breaking the forecast. The full fix for the bias itself is the annual re-estimation (stationarity-inducing priors on the offending equations — the exact precedent is the FREV/FPEXP/SFA→WN prior fix), not translation.

    The smell battery¶

    Engine: app/scripts/anchor_smell.mjs — replicates the app's exact pipeline (buildDriftCache → SENS guard → computeDriftDeltaFull, per-variable mode = the shipping default, H = 20). Per anchor:

    Test Fires when
    REDUNDANT |target − model terminal| < 0.75 pp (growth/inflation), 0.5 (EFFI), 1.0 (fiscal shares)
    SIGN_FLIP baseline first-2y stays ≥ +0.25 but the anchored path dips < −0.25 (growth/inflation)
    LOW/HIGH_WINDOW the anchored first-2y leaves the COVID-masked history envelope ± 0.5 σ and deepens/exceeds the baseline's own extreme by > 1 pp (the AND-gate tolerates NSA-seasonal dips; the COVID mask keeps the fence meaningful — unmasked, India's −110 COVID print made any dip "historical")
    EFFI_NEG the anchored effective interest rate goes negative anywhere in the window
    NC_JUMP the display shift at the first forecast quarter exceeds 2.5 pp
    NOT_EFFICACIOUS the anchored terminal fails to move toward the target (cross-drag dominates)
    CROSS_DRAG the anchor alone displaces an unanchored headline terminal (growth, inflation, EFFI) by > 1 pp

    Per-variable δc contributions are additive across targets (verified to 2 × 10⁻¹⁴), so the cross-drag attribution is exact. Verdicts are iterated on the surviving set to a fixed point, then every harmful-dropped anchor gets one Pareto-safe re-admission trial against the final set (headline macro anchors tried before fiscal shares; a candidate enters only if the entire enlarged set survives re-judging). This matters: the flagship EA HICP anchor had been killed at iteration 0 purely by the cross-drag of co-candidates (EA EFFI/FREV) that themselves died in the same iteration; re-admission recovered it (its true path vs the final set: min −0.07, terminal 3.36 → 1.53, drag on unanchored EA variables ≤ 0.28). Re-admitted: EA HICP, US EFFI, CO GDP, PL HICP.

    Results¶

    Kept: 32 anchors, 23 blocs. Headline keeps: US GDPH→2 + JGDP→2 + EFFI→5 (the flagship nominal-bias fix: nominal-growth terminal 8 → 4.3, fiscal snowball i−g −3.92 → −0.03); EA HICP→2 (terminal 3.36 → 1.53) + FPEXP→47; RU CPI→4 (model 6.55); IS CPI→2.5 (4.34); IN GDP→6.5 (8.07); SA CPI→4.5 (2.44); CR CPI→3 (0.91); UK EFFI→4 + FREV→40; CN GDP→4.3 + FREV→25.5; plus AT, BR, CA, CL, CO, FR, ID, IL, LU, MX, NO, PL, PT, TH.

    Dropped redundant — the model already lands within threshold of consensus (AU, BE, DE, DK, SE, KR, NL, ES, …). An anchor that moves nothing adds only risk.

    Dropped harmful — the correction broke the forecast. The worst offenders were exactly the blocs with the largest terminal pathologies: TR CPI (terminal 32 vs target 5), HU HICP (10.4), SK/LT/LV/SI HICP (4–7), CA/CZ/NZ/UK CPI, MY, FI, TW, GR. The referee's trim analysis (largest feasible partial correction λ) settled drop-vs-trim decisively: every feasible trim saturates the origin-jump guard — the 2.5 pp threshold becomes the shipped discontinuity — and for TR the maximal clean trim still leaves a 27 % terminal. A translation cannot fix these honestly. They are the priority queue for the Q1 re-estimation* (stationarity-inducing priors on the offending equations). Also documented: AR ships un-anchored with a residual growth-terminal pathology (0.04 vs 2.0 consensus) — its own-path-clean anchor was rejected because it alone drags unanchored AR CPI by 5.45 pp.

    Fiscal snowball coherence (terminal i−g_nom, baseline → kept set): US −3.92 → −0.03, EA −3.22 → −1.04, UK −3.44 → −1.32, CN −3.21 → −1.73, CA −1.86 → −2.22. No sign flips; the US debt path is no longer driven by an 8 % nominal-growth terminal. Side effect accepted knowingly: UK keeps FREV while FPEXP fell to cross-drag, moving the terminal primary surplus from an implausible +3.3 to ≈ +0.3 pp of GDP — toward realism.

    Known accepted edges (documented, not hidden)¶

    • IN GDP ships a soft first year (anchored NC ≈ 0.8 % for India) to fix an 8.07 → 6.5 terminal; the baseline's own NC (2.8) is itself implausible — India's near-term dynamics are a re-estimation item either way.
    • The kept US pair drags the unanchored US rate complex down (POLRATE −1.29 at terminal) — under the 1 pp headline bar for EFFI but visible; a set-cumulative, direction-aware cross-drag test is queued for the next review round, along with a primary-balance displacement gate and a spillover audit (kept δc's propagate to receiving blocs via the trend-spillover toggle, off by default).
    • NC_JUMP measures damage against the baseline origin; the honest yardstick is the nowcast (the baseline near-cast carries the same Hurwicz bias). Queued.
    • A ramped-δ engine variant (phase the correction in over ~8 quarters, zero at origin) would eliminate origin jumps as a category and could make TR/HU-class anchors shippable before re-estimation. Queued as a design option.

    What this means for users¶

    • Most economies now ship fully model-driven — what you see is the estimate, judgment-free.
    • Where an anchor ships, it fixes a documented long-run bias and its whole path stays inside the economy's own (COVID-masked) historical experience.
    • The long-run-target panel is unchanged: any anchor can still be set manually, in either solve mode (per-variable default, joint opt-in).
